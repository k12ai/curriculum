**Grade 4 AI Syllabus**

---

### **1. Intelligence Basics**

- **What is Intelligence?**
  - Understanding the concept of intelligence and what it means to be intelligent.
- **Human Intelligence**
  - Exploring how humans think, learn, and solve problems.
- **Animal Intelligence**
  - Examples of how animals display intelligence in their behaviors.

### **2. Brain and Computers**

- **Human Senses**
  - The five senses—sight, hearing, touch, taste, smell—that help us perceive the world.
- **Human Outputs**
  - Ways we express ourselves through speaking, writing, and movement.
- **Human Brain**
  - Introduction to how humans collect input through senses, process it, and provide output.
- **Basic Understanding of Computers**
  - How computers take input, process information, and provide output.
- **AI Input**
  - Formats in which AI systems receive information from the world.
- **AI Output**
  - Formats in which AI systems provide results or actions based on input.

### **3. AI in Everyday Life**

- **Introduction to AI**
  - Basic understanding of what Artificial Intelligence is.
- **Existing AI Tools**
  - **Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant**
    - Learning about voice assistants and how they assist us in daily tasks.
- **Recommendation Systems**
  - Examples of recommendation systems like Netflix or Amazon.

### **4. Basic Usage of AI**

- **What is a Prompt?**
  - Learning how to ask questions or give instructions to AI.
- **What is Context?**
  - Understanding what context means in a conversation with AI.

### **5. Popular AI Tools**

- **ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude**
  - Interacting with AI through conversation.

**Grade 8 AI Syllabus**

---

### **1. Review of Previous Topics**

### **2. Types of Learning**

- **Supervised Learning**
  - Learning from labeled datasets.
- **Unsupervised Learning**
  - Finding patterns in data without labels.
- **Reinforcement Learning**
  - Learning by trial and error using rewards and punishments.
- **One-shot Learning**
  - Recognizing patterns from a single example.

### **3. Neural Networks**

- **Neuron**
  - Understanding the basic unit of neural networks, similar to brain cells.
- **Perceptron**
  - Learning about a simple model representing a single neuron.
- **Input/Output**
  - Learning that data flows into neural networks as input and produces output results.
- **Weights**
  - Understanding values that adjust the importance of inputs in a neural network.

### **4. Types of Neural Networks**

- **Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN)**
  - AI models specialized in processing image data.
- **Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN)**
  - AI models designed for sequence data like text or time series.
- **Model Selection**
  - Understanding which model is applicable for specific use cases.

### **5. NLP (Natural Language Processing) Basics**

- **Normalization**
  - Standardizing text data for processing.
- **Stemming/Lemmatization**
  - Reducing words to their root forms.
- **Stop Word Removal**
  - Filtering out common words to focus on significant ones.
- **Tokenization**
  - Breaking text into smaller units (tokens) for analysis.

### **6. Types of Problems AI Needs to Solve**

- **Sentiment Analysis**
  - Determining the sentiment in human-written text or dialogue.
- **Emotion Recognition**
  - Identifying human emotions from facial expressions or speech.
- **Action Recognition**
  - Understanding human actions from video data.
- **Activity Recognition**
  - Interpreting activities from images or video scenes.

### **7. Large Language Models**

- **Context**
  - Understanding that AI can maintain and use information from earlier in a conversation.
- **Hallucination**
  - Learning about instances when AI generates incorrect or nonsensical information.

### **8. Use of AI in Robotics**

- **Perception**
  - Learning that robots can interpret sensory data to understand their environment.
- **Navigation**
  - Understanding that robots can plan paths and move through spaces.

### **9. Limitations of LLM (Large Language Models)**

- **Context Length**
  - Recognizing the limit to how much information AI can process at once.
- **Dependency on Detailed Prompts**
  - Understanding the need for precise instructions for accurate responses.

**Grade 9 AI Syllabus**

---

### **1. Review of Previous Topics**

### **2. Understanding of Data Types**

- **3D Point Cloud**
  - Learning about spatial data and how AI uses three-dimensional shapes.

### **3. Neural Network Training Basics**

- **Dimensions of Input Data**
  - Exploring how different types of data have various dimensions.
- **Types of Dataset**
  - Understanding and dividing data into training, validation, and test sets.
- **Structure of Dataset**
  - Learning how data is organized into features and labels for training.
- **Overfitting and Underfitting**
  - Understanding the learning quality and generalization of neural networks.
- **Learning Rate**
  - Learning about a parameter that determines the speed at which a model learns.

### **4. Types of Neural Networks**

- **Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN)**
  - AI models that generate new data resembling the training data.
- **Transformer**
  - Advanced models for processing sequences, especially in language.
- **GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)**
  - Architecture behind advanced language models like ChatGPT.

### **5. NLP Basics**

- **Parts-of-speech Tagging**
  - Identifying grammatical parts of words in sentences.
- **Vectorization**
  - Converting text into numerical vectors for processing.
- **Embeddings**
  - Representing words in a way that captures their meanings and relationships.

### **6. Large Language Models**

- **Retrieval Augmented Generation**
  - Learning about enhancing AI responses using external data sources.
- **Zero-shot Prompting**
  - Understanding how AI can perform tasks without prior specific training.
- **Fine Tuning**
  - Learning about adjusting pre-trained models for specialized tasks.
- **LLM Parameters**
  - Exploring settings like temperature, top_p, and top_k that control output randomness and creativity.

### **7. Use of AI in Robotics**

- **Manipulation**
  - Learning how robots handle and move objects.
- **Conversational Intelligence**
  - Understanding that robots can engage in human-like dialogue.
- **Task Planning**
  - Learning how robots plan and execute steps to achieve goals.

### **8. Limitations of LLM**

- **Limited Reasoning**
  - Recognizing challenges with complex problem-solving and logical reasoning.
- **Inability to Learn After Training**
  - Understanding that AI models may not update knowledge in real-time after initial training.
- **Lack of Reverse Prompting**
  - Learning about difficulties in adjusting context based on user feedback.

### **9. Types of Problems AI Needs to Solve**

- **Time Series Forecasting**
  - Predicting future data points based on past trends.
- **Pose Estimation**
  - Determining the position and orientation of objects or body parts.
- **Gesture Recognition**
  - Interpreting human gestures and movements.
- **Object Detection/Recognition/Segmentation**
  - Identifying and isolating objects within images.
- **Anomaly/Outlier Detection**
  - Finding unusual patterns that do not conform to expected behavior.
- **Weather Forecasting**
  - Using AI to predict weather conditions.
- **Reasoning**
  - Learning how AI solves problems through logical deduction and inference.

### **10. AI Development Process**

- **In-depth Review**
  - A comprehensive examination of each stage in developing AI systems.

### **11. Safe and Fair AI**

- **Socially Beneficial**
  - Ensuring AI contributes positively to society.
- **Accountability**
  - Establishing responsibility for AI decisions and actions.
- **AI Misuse**
  - Discussing ethical concerns, including AI in weapons and surveillance.
